About this website

Purpose

The unprecedented rate of spreading of COVID-19 can be frightening. In these tumultuous times panic and misinformation can spread very quickly and make matters worse for everyone. So it is important to have access not only to reliable objective data, but also to visual tools which help the average citizen interpret data about COVID-19 and understand its implications.
The purpose of this webpage is to present COVID-19 statistics and trends in visually comprehensive ways to assist the viewer in understanding the scope of the outbreak. Even data may be presented in misleading forms, so it is important to consider different visual representations before drawing conclusions. This website aims to give the public an additional visual perspective on COVID-19 data.

Data

The data featured in the visualisations on this website is fetched automatically from various open-source data repositories around the internet, including the Federal Department of Health as well as state departments. A fetch request is sent to sources every 3 hours, but the concurrency of sources is not guaranteed as each source is updated individually.
These sources include:

  • Department of Health, Australian Government
  • Center for Systems Science and Engineering, Johns Hopkins University
  • Data.NSW, NSW Government
  • health.nsw.gov.au, NSW Government
  • Our World In Data, University of Oxford, Global Change Data Lab
